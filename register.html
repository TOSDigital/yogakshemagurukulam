Summer Camp

Aatada Aramane

Cultural Summer Camp

Namaste All!! Welcome to our exclusive Cultural Summer Camp organized in Mysore for the kids aged 6-14 yrs from April10th to May 7th 2023 at Maharshi Public School.

Why Atada Aramane Summer Camp?

In ancient India we had Gurukulams where the focus of education was on 5 major aspects of child development.


The Physical, The Mental, The Intellectual, The Social and The Spiritual factors were integrally developed in a child. Gurukulams were places where children learnt and understood lifestyle in a very playful and joyful way.


What if there is a summer camp which will focus on all these 5 aspects of your child and plan their activities accordingly? Yes!! Atada Aramane is going to be a castle of joyful experience for your child because every activity will be fun-filled and a joyful cultural learning is going to happen. Our sessions on Theatre, Yoga Asanas, Yogic games, Cultural arts & crafts, Value based Story-Telling, Folk Dance, Music, Shlokas, Spoken Sanskrit are all focused on the 5 aspects.


You as a parent might find it difficult to engage your child this summer and keep them away from Mobile addiction and harmful radiations during the day. Hence this is a great opportunity to engage your child in a productive and culturally planned summer camp.


There are many activities involved in the camp which will help your child learn communication, fearlessness, team building, leadership, compassion, self-discipline and much more. Our interaction with the Nature and Tribal People will be a unique experience for your child. Your child will realize the luxury behind every simple things provided by you as parents in their life. So why think twice to provide a fun filled cultural education to your child? Hurry Bookings are open.

WEEK ONE PHYSICAL & SPIRITUAL DEVELOPMENT Yogic Lifestyle, Mindfulness, Gratitude, Leadership, Generosity, Healthy Living

WEEK THREE INTELLECTUAL DEVELOPMENT Imagination, Team Building, Communication, Transformation, Problem Solving, Perseverance

CAMP POLICY

Camp Policy, Terms & Conditions

1. The wholesome child development and theatrical camp conducted by yogakshema will commence from 10:00 am – 5:00 pm.


2. Every child will bring enough drinking water required for the whole day (3 ltrs) along with a hand towel, snacks and lunch


3. No junk food or non-vegetarian food is allowed throughout the camp


4. Fruits, salads, biscuits, sprouts, homemade snacks and any vegetarian lunch are permitted.


5. No plastic bags are allowed since the campsite will be maintained as a plastic free zone


6. No parent or guardian can interfere in decision making of a child's participation in the cultural activities conducted during the camp.


7. Dress code should be suitable for the child to participate in all the activities such as yoga, dance and games.


8. No valuable ornaments are allowed. If worn, we are not responsible for the loss of any valuables.


9. The nonrefundable camp fee should be paid in full while registration.


10. The child should bring a notebook, a pen and a pencil every day.


11. Materials required for arts and crafts will be informed a day ahead. you should make arrangements accordingly.


12. No parent will be entertained in the campsite unless and until informed or invited.


13. No leaves or holidays will be granted during the last two weeks of the camp, since they will be participating in one or the other stage programs and will be practicing for the same.


14. In case of any changes in the schedule, you will be informed in advance.


15. Once the camp fee is paid, it is non-refundable.


16. Cell phones are strictly not allowed.


POINTS TO REMEMBER

We will make all our efforts to make sure that the joyful learning in the camp is taken back home and you are able to notice the significant changes in them. Since every child is subjectively unique and different, it will take time for you to notice the changes, so give time for the child and interact with them on a regular basis.


Kindly support us by being punctual and not disturbing the child during the sessions in the camp. We respect your curiosity hence there will be a stage show on the last day of the camp. You can see your child perform and encourage him or her by giving your precious time to your child on that day.


We might have one or two educational trips organized to natural farms or forests where they will learn more about Nature and Society. This is completely optional and you can choose to take leave on those days if not willing to send the child for outdoor activities.


Dos & Don’ts

1. Help your child be Punctual by dropping and picking up the child from Campsite on time.


2. 3 days of maximum Absence is allowed during the camp. More than that, the child will not be able to participate in the stage shows.


3. Do not send any valuables, gold, mobiles or any other gadgets that might distract the children or make them feel privileged or left out.


4. Any simple Indian dress code suitable for the child to play, sit and dance is allowed.


5. Lunch and Snacks should be sent along with a hand towel and enough water for the day.


6. No packaged junk foods, pizzas, carbonated drinks or non-vegetarian lunch is allowed.


7. Homemade food and desi-snacks are allowed.


8. Any or all medical conditions (physical and metal), health issues, allergies and medications if any should be informed to the Camp Directors.


9. All materials required for art & crafts should be sent along with the child on the days prescribed.


10. A bag with, notebook, pen/pencil, eraser, sharpener and scale should be brought by the child every day


11. All arts and craft work done by your child will be on exhibition during the closing ceremony and returned to you thereafter.


12. Interact with the child every day and encourage them to share their experience or learning with the family


13. Once the camp fee is paid, it is non-refundable.
