How to keep calm

5 factors of successful life

In today's fast-paced world, it's easy to become overwhelmed and stressed out. However, it's important to take a step back and find ways to keep calm amidst the chaos. Here are some suggestions for keeping calm and collected.

Set Routines

Setting routines might sound boring, but it helps instill a sense of peace in our minds. When we have set routines, we have fewer decisions to make during the day. This frees up space in our minds for bigger and more important tasks.

Notice the Judgemental You

The harshest criticisms we experience are often self-inflicted. Nothing can stress the mind and create chaos like internal self-judgments. Pay attention to your thought patterns and notice when your inner critic makes noise. Being aware of these thoughts as they occur is the first step towards replacing criticism with calm.

No Self-Pessimism

We can’t necessarily stop ourselves from experiencing negative self-talk and beliefs, but we can distance ourselves from them. Using the phrase “I notice that I’m judging myself harshly for forgetting that file this morning” whenever we identify a negative self-judgment or belief helps us see these beliefs for what they really are: opinions, rather than facts.

Imbibe Self-Compassion

Once we are able to notice our self-criticisms and judgments in the moment, we have a chance to practice self-compassion. This means acknowledging and accepting reality, and extending the same kind of compassion to ourselves that we would to a good friend in our situation.

Write in a Journal

Writing is a great way to get our thoughts out of our heads and onto paper. Penning down our most stressful thoughts and worries each day has a similar effect to talking to someone about them. By making time to journal, you’re giving yourself the chance to process your thoughts and feelings, and to express them in a safe, private space.

In conclusion, designing your life plan is essential to achieving success and fulfillment. By demystifying your priorities, creating an idea of your future, distilling your present life, writing down your blueprint, and executing ruthlessly, you'll be well on your way to achieving your goals and living the life you've always wanted.
