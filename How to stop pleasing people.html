How to stop pleasing people?

Many of us have developed the habit of saying “yes” to everything, always trying to please others and meet their expectations. It’s easy to fall into this pattern, but it can lead to feelings of frustration and exhaustion. It’s important to learn how to say “no” to others and prioritize your own needs and goals.

Here are some tips to help you stop pleasing people and start pleasing yourself

Practice self-validation

We often seek validation from others, relying on their opinions to judge our actions. But self-validation is key to breaking the habit of pleasing people-pleasing. Take the time to reflect on what makes you feel fulfilled and act accordingly. Remember that you are the best judge of your own actions.

Embrace zero hesitation

Don’t be afraid to say “no” when you truly don’t want to do something. For example, if you don’t feel like drinking alcohol but your friends are pressuring you, it’s okay to say “no”. Start practicing with small things like this and you’ll gradually develop the courage to say “no” to bigger things too.

Take time for yourself

When someone asks you to do something, don’t feel pressured to say “yes” right away. Instead, take a moment to think about it and evaluate whether it aligns with your goals and needs. If you need more time to decide, let them know you’ll get back to them after checking your schedule. This gives you time to be with yourself and make a decision that’s right for you.

Keep a goal reminder

It’s easier to say “no” when you have a clear understanding of what you want to say “yes” to. Take time each week to evaluate your actions and see how they align with your goals. This will help you prioritize your time and energy on the things that matter most to you.

Keep toxic people away

There may be people in your life who constantly demand your time and attention, draining your energy and adding stress to your life. It’s important to recognize these toxic relationships and distance yourself from them. You don’t have to please everyone, especially those who don’t have your best interests at heart.

Remember that it’s okay to prioritize yourself and your own needs. Learning to say “no” can be empowering and help you regain control over your life. With practice and self-validation, you can break the habit of people-pleasing and start living a more fulfilling life.
