Demystifying the 6 evils of life

Have you ever felt like there are certain things holding you back from living a happy and healthy life? Perhaps you struggle with feelings of anger or jealousy, or maybe you find yourself constantly chasing after material possessions or indulging in pleasure-seeking behaviors. These are just a few examples of what is known in yogic philosophy as the six enemies of a healthy lifestyle: Kama (lust), Krodha (anger), Lobha (greed), Mada (pride), Moha (attachment), and Matsarya (jealousy).

While these qualities may seem like inherent parts of the human experience, yogic philosophy teaches us that they are actually obstacles to living a fulfilling and contented life. The good news is that with the right tools and techniques, we can learn to transform these "enemies" into more positive qualities.

Let's take a closer look at each of the six enemies and how they can be transformed :

Kama (lust)

Kama is the quality that drives us towards sensory pleasure, sexual desire, and materialistic attachment. However, if we learn to channel and tune it with yoga, it can become passion. Passion is what helps us strive for perfection in everything we do.

Krodha (anger)

Krodha is the quality that triggers violence and harmful actions. However, if we learn to manage and channel it, it can become a catalyst for non-violence and acceptance.

Lobha (greed)

Lobha is the root cause of all the excitement we get from sensory and materialistic pleasure. This pleasure, in turn, makes us accumulate more than what we need in life. However, if we learn to manage it properly, it can become responsible behavior, and as a result, all our greedy actions will vanish.

Mada (pride)

Mada is the intoxication of pride and ego in human behavior. All existing and rude attitudes emerge from this devil. However, if we learn to channel it properly, it can become humility. Humbleness is the gateway to learning and growth.

Moha (attachment)

Moha is the foolish attachment that we have towards the things that we like and want. This is the source of possessiveness and disappointment, and it can create delusion and confusion. However, if we transform and channel it, it can become equanimity and balance.

Matsarya (jealousy)

Matsarya is the quality that entangles us into misery and puts us in a cage where we cannot enjoy our own possessions and other material objects. In the name of saving, we miss the joy of living in the present. However, if we transform and channel it, it can become empathy and generosity.

These six qualities have the power to spoil the joy of life and make our lifestyle chaotic. Therefore, it is essential to transform them. The effort that we make to transform these devils into divine qualities is called Indic Wellness

Yoga and meditation are powerful tools that can help us transform these six enemies into more positive qualities. By practicing mindfulness, we can become more aware of our thoughts and emotions and learn to channel them in a positive way. By cultivating self-compassion and empathy, we can develop a deeper understanding of ourselves and others, which can lead to more fulfilling relationships and a greater sense of purpose in life.

In conclusion, the six enemies of a healthy lifestyle are not insurmountable obstacles. With the right mindset and practices, we can learn to transform these negative qualities into positive ones and live a more fulfilling and joyful life. Indic Wellness offers a path to greater self-awareness, self-compassion, and empathy, which can help us cultivate the inner peace and contentment we all seek.
